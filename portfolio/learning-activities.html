Learning Activities

In my work with children, I have found that incorporating hands-on learning activities such as cooking, American Sign Language (ASL), language development, math, music, and creative arts can be a powerful tool for engagement and retention.

In the kitchen, children can learn math skills such as measurement and fractions while also developing fine motor skills. ASL activities foster communication and language development, particularly for children who may struggle with verbal communication. Music and creative arts offer opportunities for self-expression and creativity, while also helping to build self-esteem. Overall, these activities provide a fun and interactive way for children to learn and grow.
